== Conclusion

The real promise of long-running AI systems is not that they will remember more. It is that they may eventually judge better.

That is the conceptual move worth watching. Memory is necessary, but memory alone is not the asset. The asset is a system that converts interaction into evidence, evidence into confidence, confidence into policy, and policy into better future action.

Call that persistent experience, machine judgment, or Accreted Intelligence. The label can wait. The architecture cannot.

If this direction matures, the next major layer in AI will not be defined by bigger prompts or more tools. It will be defined by the ability to compound verified judgment over time. And once that happens, warmed context will stop being just a convenience. It will become capital.

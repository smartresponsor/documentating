Evaluation

The evaluation layer explains how to read system maturity across components.

It connects the baseline vocabulary with concrete component reports and comparative atlas views.

Purpose

Evaluation is not intended to produce a single universal score.

It exists to support structured reading:

  • understanding strengths and weaknesses,

  • comparing systems on shared axes,

  • and identifying where improvement is most meaningful.

Inputs

Evaluation typically relies on:

  • component reports,

  • scorecards,

  • observable behavior,

  • and supporting evidence.

Comparative Layer

The Quality Atlas provides a cross-component view.

It should be read as a normalized snapshot rather than a final verdict.

Narrative Layer

Component reports remain the authoritative narrative layer.

They explain context, trade-offs, and reasoning behind scores.

Outcome

Evaluation produces a more structured understanding of system maturity without reducing systems to a single dimension.
